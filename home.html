WHAT IS JLPT?

The Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is a standardized test designed to gauge reading comprehension and listening skills in Japanese. It has five difficulty levels (N5 to N1).

WHO DEVISED JLPT?

The JLPT was devised by the Japan Foundation and Japan Educational Exchanges and Services in 1984.

HOW CAN I TAKE IT?

The JLPT is offered twice a year in Japan (July and December) and once or twice a year overseas. It's not available online; you have to take it on paper at a testing site.
