Welcome to Level 3 Japanese!

Konnichiwa! Welcome to your first step in learning Japanese! In Level 2, you'll be introduced to the basics of the Japanese language, including essential vocabulary, grammar structures, and cultural insights.

What do I need to know to pass JLPT N3?

N4 is very very similar to N5 but a bit tougher. First of all it is slightly longer than the N5 exam, where the N5 was 1hour 45mins, N5 is 2hours 5mins. The exam is split into 3 multiple choice papers covering vocabulary, grammar, reading and listening. There are NO speaking exams in the JLPT, and N4 onwards doesn’t have a spelling section which N5 does have.
  • Vocabulary knowledge covers:Kanji reading, the kanji for words in hiragana, using the correct word in context, and paraphrases, and usage in sentences.
  • Grammar knowledge covers:Grammar forms, sentence composition, text grammar.
  • Reading knowledge covers:Comprehension/understanding of short and mid-length paragraphs, and retrieving information.
  • Listening covers: task based understanding, key point understanding, verbal expressions and quick responses.

VOCABULARY:

The best vocabulary books out there are Nihongo Tango Speed Master. Someone has even put most of the vocabulary from this book on Memrise: JLPT N3 Standard 2400 Part 1! There are of course lots of other vocabulary and kanji courses on Memrise. Vocabulary is best to study daily alongside kanji. Of course if you come across words you don’t know you should make a note of them and study them alongside other vocabulary.

KANJI:

You may have heard of the Basic Kanji Book series as well. This is a really good series to use for studying kanji. The first books cover kanji 1-500 and the kanji based vocabulary they use. If you’ve used this serious and find it suits your learning style I suggest buying book number 3 which covers 501-750. However, you should try to avoid learning kanji on its own. It’s best to learn it in context through vocabulary, this is because you won’t be tested on the exam for individual kanji, but how they appear in vocabulary and context. I have a post on methods for Learning Kanji from Beginner to Advance. I strongly recommend these apps for kanji: Japanese Kanji Study

GRAMMER:

If you choose not to use the Nihongo Soumatome series, or would like something else on top of, then there are not many other choices for grammar. If you’re looking for free resources they are available online, but this takes a lot of work. J-Gram and Tranos has a list of grammar for the exam, but these lack on explanation, and they’re BORING. You could use it in conjunction with Tae Kim’s Guide to Grammar,Maggie Sensei,JapaneseTest4You to get better explanations.

Score Needed to Pass the Exam

For JLPT N3, the sectional pass marks are as follows:

Language knowledge (Vocabulary and Grammar): 19/60 points

Reading: 19/60 points

Listening: 19/60 points

In total, you need to achieve 90/180 points

Tips for JLPT N3 Preparation

  • Practice Hiragana and Katakana thoroughly as they form the basis of Japanese writing.
  • Focus on learning basic vocabulary and kanji characters commonly used in everyday conversations.
  • Master basic grammar points such as sentence structure, verb conjugation, and particle usage.
  • Practice listening to Japanese audio materials to improve comprehension and pronunciation.
  • Take mock exams to familiarize yourself with the format and structure of the JLPT N3 exam.

Sample Quiz for JLPT N3

  1. 1. Choose the correct particle to fill in the blank:

    彼は日本に___一年間です。

    a) から (kara)
    b) まで (made)
    c) に (ni)
    d) へ (e)

    2. Which of the following sentences is grammatically correct?

    彼が銀行に行く時、いつもお金を落とします。

    a) 彼が銀行に行く時、いつもお金を落ちます。
    b) 彼が銀行に行く時、いつもお金を落とします。
    c) 彼が銀行に行く時、いつもお金を落とします。
    d) 彼が銀行に行く時、いつもお金を落ちます。

    3. Choose the correct reading for the underlined word:

    この新聞は、毎日出版されています。

    a) しんしょ (shinsho)
    b) しんほん (shinhon)
    c) しんぶん (shinbun)
    d) しんかん (shinkan)

    4. Choose the correct polite form of the verb:

    雨が降りますので、傘を持っている方がいいです。

    a) 雨が降るので、傘を持っている方がいいです。
    b) 雨が降りていますので、傘を持っている方がいいです。
    c) 雨が降っているので、傘を持っている方がいいです。
    d) 雨が降りますので、傘を持っている方がいいです。

    5. Choose the correct kanji to complete the sentence:

    彼は昨日、新しい___を買いました。

    a) 自転車 (jitensha)
    b) 自天車 (jitensha)
    c) 自轉車 (jitensha)
    d) 自運車 (jitensha)

    6. Which of the following sentences is grammatically correct?

    この本はとても高く、買いませんでした。

    a) この本はとても高く、買いませんでした。
    b) この本はとても高かったので、買いませんでした。
    c) この本はとても高かったので、買わなかったです。
    d) この本はとても高いので、買いませんでした。

    7. Choose the correct reading for the underlined word:

    彼女は毎日、新しい本を読んでいます。

    a) しんしょ (shinsho)
    b) しんほん (shinhon)
    c) しんぶん (shinbun)
    d) しんかん (shinkan)
